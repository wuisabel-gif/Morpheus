The red pill

Keep the whole distribution

Stay for p50 / p95 / p99, the autocorrelation, and the verdict on whether the run was even long enough to trust. See how deep the tail goes — and where it detaches from the mean.

Prefill vs. decode: two different machines

Inference is two workloads glued together. Prefill ingests the whole prompt at once and saturates the GPU (compute‑bound). Decode emits one token at a time and leaves the GPU mostly idle, gated by memory bandwidth. Utilization is sampled on the same clock as every request, so the memory‑bound claim is measured, not asserted.

Why one number lies

The credibility is the product. Four methods, each a named, defensible technique from stochastic-process characterization.

Latency is correlated, not i.i.d.

Consecutive requests are coupled through KV-cache occupancy and scheduler state. Morpheus measures the autocorrelation and reports the integrated time τ_int — so 32k token events collapse to an effective sample size of a couple thousand. An i.i.d. error bar here is a lie.

Warmup is detected, not eyeballed

The transient is found by the MSER-5 changepoint rule and discarded — never a hardcoded “drop first N.”

Coordinated omission, addressed

A closed loop slows its own arrivals when the server slows, hiding the tail. --arrival-rate runs an open-loop Poisson sweep with pre-drawn arrival times, so queueing delay lands in the measurement where it belongs.

Run length is justified

An Allan-variance convergence check reports the request count at which throughput actually stabilizes — the run is as long as the statistics demand, not arbitrary.

Certify — should you believe your benchmark?

Every harness prints “p99 = 61 ms.” None tell you whether that’s a statistic or an anecdote. morpheus certify turns the stats layer into verdicts — and it referees any harness’s raw latencies, not just its own.

morpheus certify --raw results/raw

Real verdicts from the committed run: every ITL series is TRUSTED; every TTFT p99 is UNDERPOWERED at 128 requests. The benchmark grades its own numbers — and publishes the ones it doesn't yet trust, flagged.

Predict — operating points you never ran

morpheus predict fits the Universal Scalability Law to the sweep and predicts throughput at un-run concurrencies, with a bootstrap band over the raw data and a hard INTERPOLATED / EXTRAPOLATED label — because a wide band is easy to gloss over.
